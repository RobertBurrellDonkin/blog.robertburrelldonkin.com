Been a while since I've done any <Android/>.

Too long.

Too long for my old set-up on <Gentoo/> to be viable.

There's a little side-project on my mind. Maybe Android. Maybe Alexa. Perhaps neither.

So I invested some time this weekend setting back up a development environment for Android.

Turns out that the Gentoo packages are in need of a little more love. But the thing I really love about Gentoo is
that it empowers me to whatever whimsical solution suits my fancy. No two Gentoo systems are ever quite the same.

Turns out that I need some ```udev``` rules. Packages exist but the source is on
[github](https://github.com/M0Rf30/android-udev-rules/). Okay. Tried that. No joy.

But lengthy as that the rules are, they support only a few dozen devices. If - like me - you have a cheap device badged
by a second tier manufacturer, then consult <StackOverflow/> eg

https://stackoverflow.com/questions/43771918/how-do-i-set-up-udev-rules-for-debugging-a-physical-android-device-with-android

And with a custom rule, my tablet displayed my "Hello, World"
